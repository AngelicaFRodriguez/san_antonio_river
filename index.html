The San Antonio River

The San Antonio River flows 240 miles from Bexar county into the Guadalupe River in Refugio County, where it eventually drains into San Antonio Bay at the Gulf of Mexico.

Storm Water Runoff

Storm water pollution is a huge issue for our rivers and creeks. Chemicals, hazardous pollutants, bacteria, and other wastes can affect oxegen levels and nutrient balances that ecosystems depend on.
Polluted runoff also affects our drinking water. Water from our creeks and rivers naturally flows into recharge zones that we rely on. We do have systems in please that do help mitigate that risk, but water quality continues to be an important issue and priority for the overall health of our drinking water.

Please be mindful of the pollution of our drinking water
